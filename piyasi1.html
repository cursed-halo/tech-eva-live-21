Indian Astrology and Machine Learning are Correlated or Not


-Piyasi Das

ECE



I was very curious since my childhood that how a human (named astrologer)can tell my future by seeing the lines on my hand, how some stones can make a change in my life, how it is possible to predict the future events by some theory. Later I came across the term ‚ÄúAstrology‚Äù, is a kind of pseudoscience that predicts divine information about human affairs and terrestrial events by studying the positions of planets and the stars considered in their arbitrary combinations or configurations. Now I have a fair enough idea about the process of prediction or how the so called astrologers do it. But the twist happened in my vision as soon as I started my journey in Machine Learning during my third semester .I became interested in machine learning because I found that, this subject has a power of prediction through machine ,which was more acceptable for me.

Basic concept of Machine learning is to give a system the ability to learn automatically from provided data and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed. There are a good number are algorithms in ML that helps us to predict any results.I am not getting into that. In this blog am trying to tell that how I found these two fields correlated. I think Indian Astrology is a very well built and trained Machine Learning Algorithm. It seems highly impossible for a comparison between two completely irrelevant topics. But they are not indifferent. In fact, both deal with prediction of an outcome based on inputs. In Machine Learning we train our learner with training data, to estimate as closely as possible the actual mapping function. Same is the case with Astrology where people with immense experience and knowledge came together and saw a pattern in the peoples‚Äô decision making, choice of work, IQ levels, Personality traits. They used their experience of lifetime, where they saw many people and grouped them. It could also well be the initial development of taxonomy. So, let us say we believe it as a fact that astrology is a machine learning algorithm, which we did show earlier, that it is very much possible. But our machine learning algorithms strive to predict a simple classification on the modern day‚Äôs organized data, then how does astrology do it? To put it in a line, it avoids over-fitting by reducing attributes and giving probabilistic outputs.

Astrology tells us if a person lives till a certain period. It might not give a date, but an interval, which it predicts from father‚Äôs and mother‚Äôs family name, details of birth. Now, family name is a non-technical alias for a more scientific information, the genome. Its equivalent to performing a logistic regression in Machine Learning. But only with more complicated data. It then analyses the correlations on training data and forms a model. This model is subject to continuous learning.
